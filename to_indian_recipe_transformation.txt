Transformation: to Indian style cuisine

Ingredients:

- 1 small onion, cut into chunks
- 1 pound chickpea
- 1 pound chicken
- 1 tablespoon garlic  minced
- 1 teaspoon coriander
- 1 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1 teaspoon dried marjoram
- 1 teaspoon ajwain
- 1 teaspoon rosemary dried
- 1 teaspoon black freshly ground  pepper
- 1/4 teaspoon sea salt
- 12 tablespoons hummus
- 12  pita bread rounds
- 1 small head lettuce shredded
- 1 large tomato sliced
- 1 large red onion sliced
- 6 ounces paneer
- 24 tablespoons raita

Directions:

Step 1: Place onion in a food processor and finely chop.
Step 2: Transfer onion to a piece of cheesecloth and squeeze out the liquid.
Step 3: Place onion in a large bowl.
Step 4: Mix chickpea, chicken, turmeric, coriander, cumin, marjoram, ajwain, elaichi, black pepper, and salt with the onion using your hands until well mixed.
Step 5: Cover bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate until flavors blend, about 2 hours.
Step 6: Preheat the oven to 325 degrees F (165 degrees C).
Step 7: Place meat mixture in a food processor and pulse until finely chopped and tacky, about 1 minute.
Step 8: Pack meat mixture into a 7x4-inch loaf pan, ensuring there are no air pockets.
Step 9: Place the loaf pan into a roasting pan and pour enough boiling water around the loaf pan to reach halfway up the sides for a water bath.
Step 10: Bake in the preheated oven until no longer pink in the center, 45 to 60 minutes.
Step 11: An instant-read thermometer inserted into the center should read at least 165 degrees F (74 degrees C).
Step 12: Pour off any accumulated fat and cool slightly.
Step 13: Thinly slice the cooked gyro meat loaf.
Step 14: Spread 1 tablespoon hummus onto each pita bread.
Step 15: Then top each with some of the gyro meat slices, shredded lettuce, tomato slices, red onion slices, paneer cheese crumbles, and 2 tablespoons raita sauce to complete each sandwich.

Tools:
bowl, pan

Methods:
cook, bake, boil, roast
